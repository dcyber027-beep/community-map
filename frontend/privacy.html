Privacy Policy

Melbourne Community Map

Last updated: May 2026

Melbourne Community Map helps people share short-lived community safety reports, street notes, and chat messages. This policy explains the information the app uses so it can operate.

Information We Collect

  • Incident reports, street notes, chat messages, reactions, and admin-created street highlights.
  • Approximate location coordinates when you choose to use GPS, search for an address, or drop a pin.
  • Optional images attached to street notes or reports, if the feature is enabled in the app flow.
  • Anonymous session identifiers used for reactions, active-user counts, and short-lived app state.
  • Technical information sent by your browser or device when it contacts the website or API.

How We Use Information

  • To display community reports, notes, highlights, map pins, and live chat.
  • To keep the service safe by allowing administrators to review, edit, or remove content.
  • To expire short-lived reports, chat messages, and non-permanent street notes.
  • To diagnose technical issues and keep the app available.

Retention

Incident reports are designed to expire after 6 hours. Chat messages are designed to expire after 24 hours. Non-permanent street notes expire at the time selected by the person who created them. Admin-created content and permanent street notes remain until removed by an administrator.

Location And Images

Location access is optional. If you deny location permission, you can still browse the map and may use manual search or pin placement where available. Images are optional and should not include private, sensitive, or identifying information unless you have permission to share it.

Contact Details In Posts

When you create a "Helping Hand" post you can optionally add a name, phone number, or email. These details are only shown publicly if you explicitly opt in by enabling "Let people reach me". If you leave that off, your contact details are never returned to other users by the API. You can always be reached through anonymous community chat instead.

Reporting And Moderation

If you flag content using the in-app report tool, we store the report (the reason, optional details, and a reference to the content) so moderators can review it. Moderators may hide or delete content that breaks our Community Rules.

Contact

For privacy requests, support, or moderation concerns, use the contact details listed on the Support page.
