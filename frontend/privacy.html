Privacy Policy

Melbourne Community Map

Last updated: June 2026

Melbourne Community Map ("the app", "we") helps people share short-lived community safety reports, street notes, and chat messages around Melbourne. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used and shared, how long it is kept, and the choices you have. The app has no user accounts and no login for everyday use, and we do not show ads, sell your data, or use third-party advertising or cross-app tracking.

Most content you post is public. Incident reports, street notes, helping-hand posts, chat messages, reactions, and the map location attached to a post are visible to anyone using the app. Please do not post sensitive personal information, and do not post other people's information or images without their permission.

Information We Collect

  • Content you create — incident reports, street notes, helping-hand posts, chat messages, reactions, and any text you type.
  • Approximate location — the map coordinates attached to a post when you use your device GPS, search for an address, or drop a pin. Location is used only when you take one of these actions; the app does not track your location in the background.
  • Optional photos — images you choose to attach to a report or note. Images are uploaded to our image host (Cloudinary) and only the resulting image link is stored in our database. Photos you post are public.
  • Optional contact details — on a "Helping Hand" post you may add a name, phone number, or email. These are shown publicly only if you opt in (see below).
  • An anonymous session identifier — a random ID generated in your browser (not linked to your identity) used for active-user counts, reactions, and your optional live avatar marker.
  • Technical data for security — your IP address and basic request information are processed temporarily to apply rate limits and bot/abuse protection (CAPTCHA). Your IP address is not stored alongside your posts in our database.

How We Use Information

  • To display community reports, notes, highlights, map pins, live presence, and chat.
  • To geocode an address you search for, so it can be shown on the map.
  • To keep the service safe — letting moderators review, hide, edit, or remove content, and applying rate limits and CAPTCHA to prevent spam and abuse.
  • To automatically expire short-lived reports, chat messages, and temporary street notes.
  • To diagnose technical problems and keep the app available.

We do not use your information for advertising or profiling, and we do not sell or rent it.

Opt-In Contact Details In Posts

When you create a "Helping Hand" post you can optionally add a name, phone number, or email. These details are only shown publicly if you explicitly opt in by enabling "Let people reach me". If you leave that off, your contact details are never returned to other users by the app. You can always be reached through anonymous community chat instead.

How Information Is Shared

We do not sell your personal information. Besides being shown publicly in the app as described above, information is processed by the service providers we use to run the app:

  • MongoDB Atlas — database hosting (stores posts, notes, chat, reports).
  • Render — hosting for our backend API.
  • Netlify — hosting/content delivery for the website and app.
  • Cloudinary — storage and delivery of images you attach.
  • Cloudflare Turnstile — bot/abuse protection (CAPTCHA); processes your IP address and browser signals to confirm you are a human.
  • OpenStreetMap (Nominatim) — when you search for an address, the text you type is sent to OpenStreetMap's geocoding service to find coordinates.
  • OpenStreetMap / CARTO — map background tiles.

These providers process data on our behalf to operate the app, and may store or process it on servers outside Australia. We may also disclose information if required by law.

Data Retention

  • Incident reports are designed to expire about 6 hours after posting.
  • Chat messages are designed to expire about 24 hours after posting (unless an administrator pins one).
  • Temporary street notes expire at the time chosen by their author (from 1 hour up to 3 days). "Keep forever" notes and admin-created content remain until removed by an administrator.
  • Live avatar markers disappear within about a minute, and active-user presence within a few minutes, of you leaving.
  • Content reports are kept while open and for a reasonable period after they are resolved so moderators can act on repeat abuse.

Your Choices And Deleting Your Data

  • Location is optional — if you decline the permission you can still browse the map and place a pin or search manually.
  • Photos and contact details are optional — leave them out if you prefer.
  • You can remove your live avatar marker by turning your avatar off (going anonymous).
  • Much of your content deletes itself automatically when it expires.
  • To request deletion of something you posted, email communitymap@outlook.com with enough detail to identify it (for example the approximate location, time, and text of the post). Because there are no accounts, we rely on this information to locate your content.

Reporting And Moderation

If you flag content using the in-app report tool, we store the report (the reason, optional details, and a reference to the content) so moderators can review it. Moderators may hide or delete content that breaks our Community Rules.

Security

Traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit (HTTPS). Administrative actions require authentication, and we apply rate limiting and CAPTCHA to reduce abuse. No online service can be guaranteed perfectly secure, so please avoid posting information you would not want to be public.

Children's Privacy

The app is intended for a general audience and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us and we will remove it.

Changes To This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above.

Contact

For privacy requests, data deletion, support, or moderation concerns, email us at communitymap@outlook.com. You can also see the Support page.
